1. Question

    Suppose that a person suffers from two intermittent problems, one caused by their heart condition (θ1), and the other due to their diabetes (θ2). These occur with probabilities 0.4 and 0.6 respectively. When examined the person shows one of the following symptoms:

    x1: weight loss only,
    x2: feeling dizzy only,
    x3: weight loss and feeling dizzy.

    Suppose it is known by health professionals that these symptoms occur with probabilities that depend on the problem. The probabilities Pr(X=x∣θ) are given below. Construct a diagnostic rule for these symptoms and determine the probability of misdiagnosis.

    x1 x2 x3
    θ1: their heart condition 0.94 0.04 0.02
    θ2: their diabetes 0.28 0.43 0.29
